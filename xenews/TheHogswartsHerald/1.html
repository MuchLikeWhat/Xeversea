International Magical Incident – Urgent Report

Dobby the House-Elf Discovered on Indonesia’s South Sea Coast

Muggle Witnesses Report Unusual Magical Phenomena

HOGWARTS, SCOTLAND – In a shocking international development, the body of Dobby, the free house-elf and known ally of Harry Potter, has reportedly been discovered along the southern coastline of Indonesia, commonly referred to by locals as Pantai Laut Selatan.

The discovery was made by a group of Indonesian secondary school girls during what appeared to be a coastal excursion. Witnesses allegedly described finding “a small humanoid creature with large ears” lying upon the sand shortly after an unexplained magical disturbance over the sea.

Transported Across Continents

Sources within the Ministry of Magic suggest that Dobby may have been forcibly transported thousands of miles from Britain during the escalating activities of He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named. Magical residue detected along the shoreline indicates advanced Dark magic consistent with long-distance forced Apparition or magical displacement.

One senior official from the Department of International Magical Cooperation stated:

“Such a displacement would require extraordinary power. It is deeply concerning that this incident occurred outside British jurisdiction.”

Breach of Secrecy in Indonesia

The situation has become particularly delicate after reports surfaced that the Muggle students began researching their discovery online. According to insiders, several of them reportedly encountered references to “Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry” through obscure magical interference in digital systems.

The Indonesian Ministry of Magic (known locally as Kementerian Sihir Nusantara) is said to be working urgently with British authorities to contain the breach. Obliviators have been dispatched, though it is believed that at least a few fragments of memory may remain.

A confidential report suggests that one of the girls was overheard saying:

“If this is real… then Hogwarts is real too.”

International Wizarding Response

Hogwarts staff have declined to comment publicly, though students confirm that a memorial has been established near the kitchens, where Dobby once worked willingly as a paid elf.

Candlelight tributes have also been reported along Indonesia’s South Sea coast, where local magical communities are said to be quietly honouring Dobby’s bravery.

Though the Statute of Secrecy remains officially intact, this unprecedented cross-continental incident has raised concerns among wizarding authorities worldwide.

One thing remains beyond dispute:

Dobby died free.
And in death, he may have bridged two worlds.

The Hogwarts Herald will continue monitoring this developing story.
